About net worth calculator

The net worth calculator is designed to help you assess your financial situation. Based on the value of the assets and liabilities you provided, the calculator gives you the net worth result and some personal financial ratios which you can review on how good you are at managing your wealth.

The calculator offers the following features which allow you to:

  1. Download an Excel spreadsheet into your computer to help you calculate your net worth.
  2. Print out your net worth result and/or wealth management status.
  3. View and save your result in PDF format.
  4. Share the net worth calculator to your friends by email.
  5. Share the net worth calculator to your friends via Facebook.

Assumptions on calculation

The Net Worth Calculator takes account of the following into calculation:

  • Net worth is the value of all your assets minus the total of all liabilities.
  • The asset value you provided should be based on the current market value.
  • It is recommended to save an emergency fund of three to six months of living expenses to deal with any unexpected crisis. The “emergency fund” component of the chart will show 2 score or below for those whose liquidity ratio is not sufficient to cover three months’ living expenses.
  • According to the IEC Research: Knowledge, Attitudes and Behaviour towards Money and Debt Management published in April 2014, on average, Hong Kong people spent 60% of their monthly income on daily necessities. If your debt servicing ratio is equal to or higher than 40% (remainder of your monthly income after deducting daily necessities expenses), you should pay attention to managing your debt as you may find it difficult to make your ends meet. The “debt management” component of the chart will show 0 score.
  • According to the IEC Research: Knowledge, Attitudes and Behaviour towards Money and Debt Management published in April 2014, in general, Hong Kong people allocate 20% of their monthly income for savings. The “money management” component of the chart will show 2 score or below for those who have monthly savings ratio less than 20%.
